## 2. DIF Newsletter #59 — First Executive-Level Framing of Agent Identity Primacy

**Status:** DIF published its March 2026 newsletter (March 17). The Trusted AI Agents Working Group section includes the most direct statement of the book's thesis yet — from DIF's Executive Director.

**Grace Rachmany (DIF Executive Director):**
> "Agentic AI has an even stronger use case for decentralized AI than human identification."

Her reasoning: agents are ephemeral (spun up and torn down), which makes centralized identity databases impractical. DIDs fit agents more naturally than they fit humans because there's no legacy identity system competing with them.

**Working group progress reported:**
- "Agentic Authority Use Cases report" refined — concrete scenarios: agent authentication, credential presentation, secret management, operational boundaries
- Three DIF working groups (TAAWG, H&T, CAWG) now coordinating on AI-affected identity solutions
- Cross-WG collaboration: Hospitality & Travel WG engaging with TAAWG on travel industry agent use cases (aligns with the Indicio travel demo from Identosphere #165)

**Relevance:** Very high. This is the first DIF executive-level statement explicitly arguing that the decentralized identity case is *stronger* for agents than for humans. It's Shane's thesis from a different institutional angle — and it adds organizational weight to what has been a practitioner argument.

**Actionable:** The TAAWG progress section — especially "where existing DID and VC infrastructure suffices versus where new patterns are needed for agent-to-agent interactions" — is a live deliverable. Track it as a primary source for the agent-identity chapter.
