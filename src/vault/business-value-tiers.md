A four-tier scale in the [[pac-framework]] measuring why an organization would accept any risk from deploying an AI agent. Part of the [[potential-pillar]]. Without business value, there is nothing to discuss.

## The four tiers

- **V1 Incremental**: saves time on existing tasks. Useful, but easily replicated by any competitor. Most organizations start here and stay here.
- **V2 Operational**: changes how work gets done. Removes bottlenecks, enables new workflows. Creates process advantage.
- **V3 Strategic**: creates competitive advantage. The agent does something competitors cannot easily copy. Network effects, proprietary data, unique context infrastructure.
- **V4 Transformative**: enables entirely new business models. The agent is the product, not a feature within a product.

## The interesting question

What infrastructure investments move an organization from V1 toward V3 and V4?

The answer from the [[pac-framework]]: context infrastructure and evaluation pipelines. Context appreciates with every model upgrade — the better the model, the more value you extract from well-structured context. Evaluation pipelines let you know when agents are working and when they are failing, which is the prerequisite for earning higher [[autonomy-levels]].

V3 and V4 require the inferential edge: the gap between having access to a powerful model and being able to use it safely at scale. Organizations that close it first gain compounding advantage — every automated process improves their context pipelines, trust infrastructure, and operational learning.
