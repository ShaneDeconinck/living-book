## Patterns for Ghosty

**1. Double-colon sentences.**
The draft had: "And the inferential edge: the organizational readiness to let powerful models run safely: compounds with every month of head start." Two colons in one sentence break reading. When you are using colons to avoid em dashes (correct per house style), you cannot use one to introduce an appositive mid-sentence and another later. Split into two sentences instead.

**2. "All converging simultaneously" — converging already implies simultaneously.**
Cut the redundancy. "The standards, regulations, and infrastructure for agent governance are all converging simultaneously" becomes "are converging." Trust the verb.

**3. Unsourced superlatives need footnotes or removal.**
"Microsoft is shipping the first major agent governance control plane" had no footnote and used "first major" — an unverifiable superlative. Either source it precisely or soften. Unverifiable superlatives in an otherwise well-cited chapter undermine the whole.

**4. Long colon-list sentences work better with semicolons.**
The Sardine sentence listed three attack types in a single run-on after a colon. Using semicolons between list items inside a sentence makes each item land separately. It is not a list change — it is punctuation doing its job.

**5. "Rapidly" adds nothing when context is urgent throughout.**
"The intelligence is rapidly becoming commodity" — the entire chapter establishes urgency. "Rapidly" here is decoration. Cut it.
